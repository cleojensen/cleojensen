Jamiroquai

Funk. Soul. Culture.

About the Band

Jamiroquai is a British funk and acid jazz band led by Jay Kay. Known for their groove-heavy sound and unique visual identity, the band blends funk, disco, and soul into a modern style that still feels rooted in the past.

Emergency on Planet Earth

Jamiroquai’s debut album stands out because of its strong political messaging. It focuses on environmental destruction, corporate greed, and the direction of society. Songs challenge listeners to think about their role in harming or protecting the planet.

Released in the early 1990s, the album reflects a growing awareness of environmental issues. It helped define Jamiroquai as more than just a music group—they were using funk as a platform for meaningful ideas.

Influences

Jamiroquai’s music is inspired by funk, disco, and soul traditions, especially from the 1970s. Their sound reflects a transition from older analog styles into more modern production, while still keeping live instrumentation at its core.
